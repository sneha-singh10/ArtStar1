Learn how to clean, store, and maintain your paintbrushes to keep them performing their best for years.

1. Why Brush Care Matters

Proper brush care ensures smoother painting, longer brush life, and consistent paint flow. Neglecting your brushes can lead to stiff bristles and ruined shapes ‚Äî making your painting harder and less enjoyable.

2. Cleaning After Each Use

Cleaning your brushes immediately after painting prevents paint from drying inside the bristles and damaging them.

  • Watercolor Brushes: Rinse thoroughly with clean water.
  • Acrylic Brushes: Wash with mild soap and warm water before the paint dries.
  • Oil Brushes: Use turpentine or linseed oil, then wash with soap and water.

3. Avoid Harsh Conditions

Never leave brushes standing in water or solvent for long periods ‚Äî it bends the bristles and loosens the glue that holds them.

  • Lay brushes flat or at a slight angle when drying.
  • Use gentle pressure when washing ‚Äî don‚Äôt crush the bristles.
  • Clean handles and ferrules (metal parts) regularly to avoid paint buildup.

4. Reshaping the Bristles

After washing, gently reshape the brush tip with your fingers to maintain its original form. For fine brushes, you can dip them lightly in hair conditioner or brush restorer and shape them before storing.

  • Gently squeeze out excess water with a towel.
  • Use your fingers to restore the natural brush tip.
  • Avoid strong twisting or bending motions.

5. Proper Storage

Once completely dry, store brushes upright with the bristles facing up ‚Äî this keeps them in shape and prevents moisture buildup.

  • Use a brush holder or jar for upright storage.
  • Keep away from direct sunlight or heat sources.
  • If traveling, use a roll-up brush case to protect bristles.

6. Deep Cleaning (Weekly or Biweekly)

For frequent painters, deep cleaning helps remove hidden paint and residue that daily cleaning might miss.

  • Use specialized brush cleaners or mild shampoo.
  • Massage gently and rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water.
  • Repeat until the water runs clear.

7. Extending Brush Life

With care, even inexpensive brushes can last a long time. Remember: the cleaner your brushes, the cleaner your strokes!

  • Never mix different paint types (like oil and acrylic) on the same brush.
  • Use older brushes for rough textures or backgrounds.
  • Rotate your brushes so they wear evenly.
