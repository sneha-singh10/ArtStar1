1. Understanding the Color Wheel

The color wheel is the foundation of color theory. It consists of primary colors (red, yellow, blue), secondary colors (green, orange, purple), and tertiary colors formed by mixing them.

2. Warm and Cool Colors

Warm colors (red, orange, yellow) evoke energy and warmth. Cool colors (blue, green, violet) create calmness and depth. Use a balance of both to control mood and contrast.

3. Color Harmony

Color harmony refers to pleasing color combinations, such as:

  • Complementary: Opposite on the color wheel (e.g., blue & orange).
  • Analogous: Side-by-side colors (e.g., yellow, yellow-green, green).
  • Triadic: Three evenly spaced colors (e.g., red, blue, yellow).

4. Value and Saturation

Value refers to how light or dark a color is. Saturation shows how vivid or dull a color appears. Mastering these helps create realistic lighting and depth in your paintings.

5. Try It Yourself!

Create a small color wheel using paints you have. Mix primary colors to form secondary and tertiary shades ‚Äî observe how they change with light.
