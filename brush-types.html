Discover different types of brushes, their shapes, and how to use them to bring your art to life.

1. Round Brush

The round brush is one of the most versatile tools. Its pointed tip makes it perfect for outlining, fine details, and filling in small areas.

  • Excellent for sketching and controlled strokes.
  • Used in watercolor and acrylic painting.
  • Can create both thin and thick lines depending on pressure.

2. Flat Brush

Flat brushes have a squared end with medium to long hairs. They‚Äôre ideal for bold, sweeping strokes and sharp edges.

  • Useful for blending large areas or creating textures.
  • Can be turned sideways for fine lines.
  • Often used for acrylic and oil painting.

3. Filbert Brush

The filbert brush is flat with rounded edges ‚Äî a mix between a flat and round brush. It‚Äôs great for blending soft edges and natural shapes like petals or skin tones.

  • Ideal for smooth blending.
  • Gives soft, rounded edges in your strokes.
  • Common in portrait and landscape painting.

4. Fan Brush

Fan brushes are spread out in a fan shape, making them perfect for creating textures such as grass, leaves, or clouds.

  • Used for blending and texturing.
  • Ideal for painting natural landscapes.
  • Best used with light touch and minimal paint.

5. Detail or Liner Brush

Detail brushes are thin and long, designed for intricate details, lettering, or final touch-ups.

  • Used for outlines and small highlights.
  • Essential for fine detailing in portrait art.
  • Helps add finishing touches to paintings.

6. Mop Brush

The mop brush has soft, thick bristles, ideal for blending and softening colors ‚Äî especially in watercolor painting.

  • Creates smooth gradients and washes.
  • Useful for covering large areas softly.
  • Excellent for sky or background blending.

7. Cleaning and Caring for Brushes

Proper brush care extends the life of your tools and ensures better performance.

  • Always clean brushes immediately after use.
  • Use mild soap and lukewarm water.
  • Store brushes vertically with bristles facing upward.
